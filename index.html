About

A connector at the centre of government technology.

I work as a Business Relationship Manager at HM Treasury, partnering with the Government Actuary's Department, the Office for Budget Responsibility and the National Infrastructure and Service Transformation Authority. The job, done properly, is translation: sitting between the people who need things and the people who build them, and making sure change is driven by the business rather than done to it.

Departments rarely struggle because the technology is bad. They struggle because nobody owns the relationship between ambition and delivery. That relationship is what I manage, from everyday service demand through to how the Treasury Group approaches AI adoption, including directing the Treasury Innovation Day and the Digital Excellence Series.

The route here was not typical. I grew up in care in County Durham, and joined the Treasury's new Darlington Economic Campus through the Civil Service Care Leaver Internship Scheme two days after graduating. Within three years I was ranked 6th in the public sector in the AI100 UK, named in Apolitical's Government AI 100, and recognised as a Global Top BRM by the BRM Institute.

That background is not a footnote to the technology work. Growing up inside systems designed around you, rather than with you, teaches you exactly what to look for when governments start automating decisions. It is why I chair the Cross-Government Care Leavers Network, why I publish research on social mobility policy, and why accessibility runs through everything I do professionally.

I write GOV×BRM, a biweekly newsletter on business relationship management in government read by over 700 practitioners in the UK and internationally, and I am a Professional Member of BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT.

How Apprenticeship Providers Underrepresent Care Leaver Eligibility: A Systematic Audit of Funding Exemption Guidance Across the APAR Register

A systematic audit of 107 apprenticeship providers, finding that 64% made no reference to care leaver or EHC-related funding support at the point where prospective apprentices seek eligibility information. Sets out five policy recommendations, with the full compliance dataset published openly.
