About

I grew up in care in County Durham. Two days after graduating from Northumbria University, I started at HM Treasury's new Darlington Economic Campus through the Civil Service Care Leaver Internship Scheme. I have been there since, now as a Business Relationship Manager in Treasury Business Solutions.

The job, done properly, is translation. Departments do not struggle with technology because the technology is bad; they struggle because nobody owns the relationship between the people who need things and the people who build them. That relationship is what I manage, from everyday service demand through to how the department approaches AI.

Outside the day job I co-founded and co-chair the cross-government Care Leavers' Network, which supports care-experienced civil servants and widens the routes into public service. That background is not a footnote to the technology work. Growing up inside systems that were designed around you, rather than with you, teaches you exactly what to look for when governments start automating decisions.

I write GOVBRM, a newsletter on business relationship management in government, and completed TechUP's AI Foundations programme with a capstone workshop on ethical AI in the public sector.
