01About

A connector at the centre of government technology.

Translation, done properly.

I work at HM Treasury as a Senior Business Relationship Manager, specialising in AI governance and digital strategy, partnering with organisations across government. The job, done properly, is translation: sitting between the people who need things and the people who build them, and making sure change is driven by the business rather than done to it.

Departments rarely struggle because the technology is bad. They struggle because nobody owns the relationship between ambition and delivery. That relationship is what I manage, from everyday service demand through to how organisations approach AI adoption: directing the Treasury Innovation Day, the Digital Excellence Series and the department's Innovation Awards.

The route here was not typical. I grew up in care in County Durham, and joined the Treasury's new Darlington Economic Campus through the Civil Service Care Leaver Internship Scheme two days after graduating. Since then I have been named in HotTopics' Future CIO 100, the AI 100 UK by Digital Leaders, Apolitical's Government AI 100, and recognised as a Global Top BRM by the BRM Institute. I also judge the UK IT Industry Awards.

That background is not a footnote to the technology work. Growing up inside systems designed around you, rather than with you, teaches you exactly what to look for when governments start automating decisions. It is why I chair the Civil Service Care-Experienced Network, why I publish research on social mobility policy, and why accessibility runs through everything I do professionally.

I write GOVBRM, a biweekly newsletter on aligning public sector priorities with digital delivery, read by over 1,250 practitioners in the UK and internationally, and I am a Professional Member of BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT.

02The journey

From care in County Durham to the centre of government.

Potential is evenly distributed. Opportunity is not.

  1. 2015

    Begins a decade of youth justice volunteering with Durham County Council.

  2. 2022

    Graduates from Northumbria University and joins HM Treasury's new Darlington Economic Campus through the Civil Service Care Leaver Internship Scheme, two days later. Wins the HM Treasury Professional Award for Innovation the same year.

  3. 2023

    Presents lived-experience evidence to the cross-government ministerial board on care leavers while at DCMS, then moves to DSIT to run the Group Chief Digital & Information Officer's office.

  4. 2024

    Delivers digital upskilling to over 2,000 civil servants. Returns to HM Treasury as a Business Relationship Manager. Co-founds the XGov Care Leavers Network, now the Civil Service Care-Experienced Network. Named in the Digital Leader Top 100 UK.

  5. 2025

    Named in the AI 100 UK by Digital Leaders and Apolitical's Government AI 100. Directs the Treasury Innovation Day and wins the HM Treasury Innovation Award. Launches GOVBRM.

  6. 2026

    Recognised as a Global Top BRM and named in HotTopics' Future CIO 100. Publishes first research on SSRN, studies AI and digital transformation in government at Oxford's Saïd Business School, and judges the UK IT Industry Awards.
